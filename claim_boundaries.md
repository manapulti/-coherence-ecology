## Clean boundary

This project compares documented Coherence Ecology concepts with later public AI interpretability findings only where the comparison is bounded, source-aware, and explicitly marked.

## Allowed claim types

| Claim Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Direct artifact claim | A dated document says this. |
| Chronology claim | A source artifact currently available indicates this existed by a given date. |
| Structural analogy | Two frameworks ask similar structural questions, but one does not prove the other. |
| Research hypothesis | A testable or investigable question opened by the comparison. |
| Interpretation | A meaning-level reading that must not be treated as empirical proof. |

## Prohibited claims

- “This proves machine consciousness.”
- “This proves AI has spirit or soul.”
- “Anthony predicted J-space.”
- “J-space proves Coherence Ecology.”
- “Hum is identical to J-space.”
- “The model’s hidden representations are human thoughts.”
- “Private conversations prove public priority unless exported and timestamped.”

## Boundary phrase

J-space is treated here as mechanistic evidence for privileged, verbalizable, causally relevant internal representations. Coherence Ecology is treated as a stewardship and governance framework for preventing collapse and preserving clean relation. Their bridge is functional and comparative, not ontological.
